# RRF Benchmark Results — Initial Evaluation

## Purpose

This document aggregates results from multiple reasoning tests to demonstrate how the Ralone Recursive Framework (RRF) evaluates output quality.

---

---

## Observations

### 1. Weak Models Fail in Two Patterns

* introduce unnecessary uncertainty
* ignore critical conditions

---

### 2. Strong Outputs Share Traits

* precise conclusions
* correct use of conditions
* no unnecessary hedging

---

### 3. Failure Types Detected by RRF

#### RTS Failures

* factual errors
* overgeneralization

#### RLGE Failures

* broken reasoning chains
* missing logical links

#### RVE Failures

* unsupported claims
* contradiction with known reality

---

## Key Insight

RRF does not measure intelligence directly.
It measures whether reasoning is:

* correct
* grounded
* verifiable

---

## Conclusion

Even simple tests show clear separation between:

* high-quality reasoning
* weak or flawed reasoning

RRF provides a structured way to detect this difference reliably.
